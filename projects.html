Edfund is a work in progress project, a website intended to help college students with their tuition fees. In this age, education has become more important than ever. However, unfortunately, it has also become increasingly difficult for people from lower income families to gain access.

Disclaimer : This is an early concept. What is being shown and discussed can be susceptible to change in the final product.



Edfund will allow students to setup their profiles in which they can describe in detail about their lives, why they are in this situation.
Donors can browse through the recipients and make donations with ease.
To prevent fraud, users will have to prove their identity with government documents such birth certificates, educational backgrounds and accomodation records.

Amplify the chaos, fun and action by ten folds. Then you get SP Air Hockey station!



SP Air Hockey was built from scratch by a team of ten polytechnic students. With the initial intention being designing a game station like in arcade game centers.
After several meetings and a few prototypes, we came up with the idea to modify the existing air hockey game.


Once the goal was set, we proceeded to divide the responsibilty to each team member with me becoming programmer for the team.
It was a year long project which means we were required to work on the project for the whole academic year in addition to the lectures.

As a programmer, I worked with Programmable Logic Controllers to control the all the electrical and mechanical components that included in the station (such as solenoids, LED light strips, timers, fans, buttons and power supplies etc.) This was a fantastic opportunity for me to apply everything that I have learned in the first two years of college to practical use. We used Panasonic PLC together with FPWIN Pro 6 software from programming.

We were awarded gold medal prize in the Engineering.
